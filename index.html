About me

Ph.D. in mathematics from the Universidad de Chile. My advisor was Nicolás Libedinsky. My research focuses on Representation theory of Hecke algebras, Soergel bimodules, Categorification, and Combinatorics. Additionally, I am keenly interested in algorithms, graphical representations of data, and their topological structures. Find my CV here.

I am grateful for the support provided by the National Research and Development Agency (ANID).

I graduated with honors in Mathematics from the Universidad de Chile and pursued my master's degree at IMPA in Brazil under the guidance of Milton Jara.

Outside of academia, I enjoy cycling and running. During the warmer months in Santiago, you can find me engaging in these sports at San Cristobal hill. When circumstances keep me indoors, I devote my free time to coding for personal projects. Furthermore, I have a passion for learning languages, allowing me to enjoy audiovisual content in its original form. Lastly, I adhere to a vegetarian lifestyle.
